Stop marketing to everyone. Start marketing to the right wallets.

Wallet segmentation is how you identify and target your most valuable users. With Formo, you can slice your audience by net worth, lifecycle stage, onchain behavior, and more, then export those segments for campaigns on X, Farcaster, and Lens.

## Prerequisites

- Formo is installed on your dapp and collecting data
- You have a **Growth+ plan** to use saved segments
- Users page is accessible from your sidebar

## Part 1: Start on the Users Page

Open Formo's dashboard and click **Users** in the sidebar. You'll see a table of all users with their wallet addresses, lifecycle stages, net worth, labels, and last activity timestamp.

Each row is a wallet. Every column is filterable. This is your segmentation starting point.

## Part 2: Apply Property Filters

Click the **Filter** button to add conditions. You can filter by:

- **Lifecycle**: New, Returning, Power User, Churned, Resurrected
- **Net Worth**: Exact ranges ($0-$10k, $10k-$100k, $100k+, custom)
- **Wallet Labels**: Verified accounts, sanctions flags, passport scores
- **Apps Used**: Filter to users who used specific dapps
- **Tokens Held**: Filter by specific token holdings
- **Chains**: Filter by blockchain activity (Ethereum, Polygon, etc.)
- **Device**: Mobile, desktop, tablet
- **Country**: Geographic filtering

For example, to target whale prospects, filter for net worth > $100k AND lifecycle = New.

## Part 3: Apply Attribution Filters

Under **Attribution** section, filter by:

- **UTM Source / Medium / Campaign**: First-touch or last-touch attribution
- **Referrer**: How users arrived at your dapp
- **First Touch vs Last Touch**: Distinguish acquisition vs conversion sources

Example: Show me all users from a specific Twitter campaign (utm_source=twitter) who had at least one transaction.

## Part 4: Build Behavioral Segments

Click the **Behavioral** tab to create segments based on user actions.

Define conditions like: "Users who performed Event X at least N times in the last Y days"

Combine multiple behaviors with AND/NOT logic:
- Users who completed at least 2 swaps AND connected a wallet
- Users who visited the app 5+ times AND did NOT make a transaction

These filters are powerful for identifying power users vs disengaged users.

## Part 5: Save as a Segment

Once your filters are set, click the **Save as Segment** button in the top right. Give it a name like "Q1 Whale Prospects" or "Churned Power Users".

Saved segments appear in a dropdown menu for quick reuse across the app. You can apply the same segment to multiple dashboards and reports.

## Part 6: Export Your Segment

With your segment active, click **Export as CSV** in the top right.

Formo downloads a CSV file with columns: address, ENS name, net worth, lifecycle stage, last activity, and more. This is campaign-ready data.

## Part 7: Use Exported Data for Campaigns

Import the CSV into your marketing tools:

- **X/Twitter**: Upload to Tailwind, Buffer, or Twitter Ads for wallet-based targeting
- **Farcaster**: Use Neynar or Warpcast's audience upload feature
- **Lens**: Import to Lens' native campaign tools
- **Email**: If you have wallet owners' emails, import for email campaigns

Each exported wallet is a real, verified onchain identity with transaction history.

## 5 Ready-to-Use Segment Recipes

### Recipe 1: Whale Prospects

Target high-net-worth users who are brand new.

Filter config:
- Net Worth: > $100,000
- Lifecycle: New
- Sessions: = 1

Expected size: 2-5% of your user base (highly valuable)

### Recipe 2: Churned Power Users

Re-engage users who were once active but disappeared.

Filter config:
- Lifecycle: Churned
- Sessions: > 10
- No Activity: > 30 days

Expected size: 5-15% of your user base

### Recipe 3: Campaign Converts

Identify users who arrived from a specific campaign and converted.

Filter config:
- UTM Source: [your campaign source]
- Last Touch: [same campaign]
- Behavioral: Completed Transaction at least 1 time

Expected size: Variable, depends on campaign

### Recipe 4: DeFi Crossover

Find users who use competitor dapps and yours (cross-protocol opportunity).

Filter config:
- Apps Used: [Competitor App A] AND [Your App]
- Behavioral: Performed swap at least 1 time in your app

Expected size: 10-20% of your user base

### Recipe 5: Dormant High-Value

Wake up large wallets that haven't returned recently.

Filter config:
- Net Worth: > $50,000
- Lifecycle: Churned OR Returning
- Last Activity: > 14 days ago

Expected size: 5-10% of your user base

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="What's the difference between saved segments and filters?">
Filters are temporary. They disappear when you reload the page. Saved segments persist and can be reused across dashboards, funnels, and reports. Growth+ plans can save unlimited segments.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I combine segments with other segments?">
Yes. Apply a saved segment, then add additional filters on top. The results will be the intersection of both.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How often do wallet metrics like net worth update?">
Net worth and wallet labels update daily. Behavioral data (sessions, last activity) updates in real time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I export segments to my email provider?">
Yes, if you've collected email addresses alongside wallet addresses. Export the CSV and import it into Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or your email platform's upload feature.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need Growth+ to use segments?">
Yes. Growth+ is required to save segments. Free and Pro plans can still filter and export, but can't save segment configurations for reuse.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's the maximum segment size I can export?">
There is no hard limit. Export times vary by size (100k wallets typically takes 30-60 seconds). Contact support for bulk exports larger than 1M wallets.
</Accordion>
