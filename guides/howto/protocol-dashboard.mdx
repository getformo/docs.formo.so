A dashboard is your protocol's cockpit. It's where you see everything that matters at a glance: user growth, transaction volume, revenue trends, and whether your acquisition channels are working. Let's build one.

## Prerequisites

- A Formo account with a connected project
- At least 7 days of data flowing through your [SDK](/sdks/web) or [contract events](/guides/howto/contract-events)
- Edit access to your project dashboard

## Part 1: Create Your Dashboard Board

Navigate to **Dashboards** in the sidebar. Click **Create Board** in the top right.

Name your board "Protocol Health" (or whatever makes sense for your protocol). This is your custom dashboard page where all charts will live.

## Part 2: Add Key Metrics Chart

Click **Create Chart** on your empty board. In the chart builder:

1. Select **Line Chart** (or Bar for comparison)
2. Choose your metric: Unique Visitors, Wallet Connects, or Transactions
3. Set your time range (last 30 days is standard)
4. Group by Day

This chart shows your protocol's heartbeat. Are users growing? Are transaction volumes stable or declining?

**What to track:**
- Unique Visitors (DAU/WAU/MAU)
- Wallet Connects per day
- Transaction count per day

## Part 3: Add Traffic Sources Chart

Create another chart:

1. Select **Bar Chart**
2. Metric: Unique Visitors or Transactions
3. Dimension: Referrer (or UTM Source if tracking campaigns)
4. Sort by value, descending

This shows you where your users come from. Which channels drive the most traffic? Which convert best?

## Part 4: Add Funnel Chart

Create a new chart:

1. Select **Funnel** chart type
2. Define your funnel steps:
   - Step 1: Page view (using the `page` event type)
   - Step 2: Connect wallet (using the `connect` event type)
   - Step 3: First Transaction
   - Step 4: Repeat Transaction (optional)
3. Set your time range (30 days)

This chart answers the hardest question: how many users actually convert? Where do people drop off?

## Part 5: Add Retention Chart

Create another chart:

1. Select **Retention** chart type
2. Choose your cohort dimension: Sign-up Date or First Transaction Date
3. Metric: Unique Users returning
4. Set retention windows: D1, D7, D14, D30, D90

Retention is the metric most protocols ignore until they need it. This chart shows whether users stick around or ghost after their first transaction.

## Part 6: Add User Path Chart (Sankey)

Create another chart:

1. Select **User Path** chart type (Sankey diagram)
2. Configure: Page (or custom event) flows
3. Limit to top 5-10 paths for clarity

This shows the most common user journeys through your protocol. Do users go straight to swap? Do they explore first? Which paths lead to transactions?

## Part 7: Add Custom SQL Chart

For advanced metrics (daily revenue, top wallets, gas spent), use a custom SQL chart:

1. Click **Create Chart** and select **SQL Query**
2. Open the [Explorer](/guides/howto/sql-explorer) to build and test your query first
3. Paste your tested SQL into the chart builder
4. Select your chart visualization (Line, Bar, Pie)

Example: Daily revenue by tracking transaction values from contract events.

## Part 8: Arrange Your Dashboard

Once all charts are created:

1. Drag charts to reorder them
2. Resize charts (larger for important metrics, smaller for supporting data)
3. Group related charts together (top row: key metrics, middle: funnel and retention, bottom: traffic sources)
4. Save your layout

A well-arranged dashboard tells a story: growth, conversion, retention, and acquisition all in one view.

## Part 9: Share Your Dashboard

Click **Share** in the top right:

- Copy the public link to share with stakeholders
- Invite team members and set permissions (viewer, editor)
- Set auto-refresh intervals if you need real-time updates

Your team can now check protocol health anytime without asking for a report.

## 5 Essential Dashboard Charts Every Protocol Needs

| Chart | Metric | Why It Matters |
|-------|--------|---|
| **Key Metrics Line Chart** | DAU, Transactions | Your protocol's growth trajectory |
| **Funnel Chart** | Pageview → Connect → Transaction | Where users drop off and your biggest opportunity |
| **Retention Chart** | D7, D30 cohort retention | Whether users actually come back (stickiness) |
| **Traffic Sources** | Referrer breakdown | Best acquisition channels to double down on |
| **User Path (Sankey)** | Common flows | How real users navigate your protocol |

## Pro Tips

<Tip>
**Benchmark retention.** Most DeFi protocols see D7 retention between 5-15%. If yours is 20%+, you have exceptional product-market fit.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Watch for seasonal patterns.** Most protocols show dips on weekends and during bear markets. Use 90-day views to spot real trends beneath the noise.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Compare channels.** A channel with lower volume but higher conversion rate is worth more than a high-volume channel with poor conversion. Funnel by UTM source.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Set up alerts.** Create alerts in Project Settings for critical metrics (DAU drop >20%, conversion rate below threshold). See [Alerts guide](/guides/howto/alerts) for details.
</Tip>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How often do dashboards update?">
    Charts update every 5-10 minutes with new data. For real-time metrics, check the Activity page which updates second-by-second.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I schedule dashboard reports?">
    Not yet, but you can share your dashboard link with stakeholders or export individual charts. Email reports are on our roadmap.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a Board and a Chart?">
    A Chart is a single visualization (line, bar, funnel, etc.). A Board is a collection of charts arranged on one dashboard page. You can create multiple boards for different purposes (Protocol Health, Marketing Performance, Wallet Intelligence, etc.).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I filter dashboard charts by date range?">
    Yes. Each chart has its own date range setting. You can also add filters at the board level to filter all charts at once (filtered views coming soon).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How far back can I view historical data?">
    You can view data for as long as your project has been collecting it (usually from your first SDK install). Most protocols have 3-12 months of data. Older data can be queried via the Explorer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my dashboard is loading slowly?">
    Dashboards with 10+ complex charts may load slower. Try breaking your analysis into multiple boards (one for acquisition, one for monetization, etc.) or simplifying chart queries.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I compare dashboards across different projects?">
    Not yet. However, you can view insights across all projects from your Workspace. For cross-project analysis, use the Explorer to write custom SQL queries.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
